PHD Candidates, Spring 2013

David Rosenberg

Since coming to Brooklyn College in Fall 2010, David has been a TA in Biology, a Project Leader and Treasurer, founding president of Students for Food Sustainability, co-chair of the college's first Participatory Budgeting committee, director of the Auxiliary Enterprise corporation, a senior organizer with New Yorkers against fracking, and for the last year the Speaker of the CLAS Assembly. Since becoming Speaker, the Assembly has more transparent rules, an updated constitution, an open budget, and two new committees for outreach and engagement. During Hurricane Sandy, David led the school's relief efforts, making us the first and fasted CUNY school to respond. David is a first-generation American and, upon graduating, will be the first in his family to receive a college degree. He plans to go to law school and start a career in public service.

Ayala Lieberman

Since coming to Brooklyn College as a Presidential Scholar, Ayala has worked as a peer-mentor, as well as a mentor to women pursuing careers in the sciences through WISE. Last year, she was elected as an independent candidate, and currently serves as the Assembly's deputy speaker. After graduating, Ayala plans on pursuing a career in Dentistry.

In the past, she has worked as a tutor at the BC Learning Center as well as a peer lab instructor in the R.I.S.E. program to encourage and support lower classmate in doing undergraduate science research.
